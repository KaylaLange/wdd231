Featured Attraction

The Stanley Hotel

Address: 333 E Wonderview Ave

Time to spend: Less than 1 hour

The grand Stanley Hotel, a Historic Hotels of America member and listed on the National Register of Historic Places, opened in 1909 as a luxurious retreat for East Coast vacationers in Estes Park. Known for its historic significance and as the inspiration for the Overlook Hotel in Stephen King's "The Shining," the Stanley also has a history of paranormal activity. Visitors can explore this history through daytime tours focused on pop culture or nighttime tours highlighting ghost lore. Past travelers praise the tour guides for their storytelling and interesting tidbits.

Did You Know?

  • Estes Park was named for Joel Estes and his wife Patsy, who traveled from Kentucky to become the first permanent residents.
  • The layout of the town was established within the first decade of its founding and remains mostly intact to this day.
  • The Stanley Hotel, built in 1909, was the first hotel in Estes Park and would later go on to be featured in the hit box office film, The Shining.
